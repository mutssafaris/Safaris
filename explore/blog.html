Journal from the Bush: Why We Write

Welcome to the Muts Safaris journal—a collection of stories, guides, and reflections from the field, written by travelers for travelers. Our blog isn't just a repository of tips; it's a living record of what we've learned from years of moving through Kenya's wild places. Here you'll find practical advice on when to visit for the best migration sightings, deep dives into cultural etiquette with Maasai communities, packing lists refined by experience, and personal narratives that capture the ineffable magic of moments like watching a newborn zebra take its first steps or sharing tea with a Samburu elder.

We believe that informed travelers make better travelers—and more importantly, they become advocates for the places they love. Our articles blend firsthand experience with expert knowledge from our Maasai guides, wildlife biologists, and local partners. Whether you're planning your first safari or your twentieth, we invite you to dive in, get curious, and let these stories fuel your own African adventure.

"Some journeys are meant to be lived; others are meant to be shared. These stories are our gift to you—to inspire, to prepare, and to keep the spirit of Africa alive until you can return."
